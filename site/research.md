My doctoral research at the University of Sheffield focuses on making modern speech and language technology accessible to endangered and low-resource languages. I work primarily with **Manx Gaelic (Gaelg)**, a Celtic language from the Isle of Man, but the methods I develop are designed to be language-agnostic and transferable.

## Research interests

**Low-resource automatic speech recognition** — Building ASR systems that can bootstrap from very small corpora (tens of minutes to a few hours of audio), using techniques like semi-supervised learning, data augmentation, and cross-lingual transfer from related languages.

**Diffusion-based voice conversion** — Developing Grad-VC, a voice conversion framework built on Grad-TTS and DiffVC. The system uses word-pair supervision for non-parallel training, removing the dependency on forced alignment that makes standard approaches impractical for low-resource languages. [Listen to sample training pairs](/research/pair-corpora/).

**Endangered language speech synthesis** — Training multi-speaker TTS systems for Manx Gaelic with limited speaker data, focusing on naturalness and speaker similarity in a setting where more data cannot simply be collected.

**Multilingual and Celtic language modelling** — Cross-lingual transfer learning across Celtic languages (Irish, Welsh, Scottish Gaelic, Manx, Cornish, Breton) and other endangered language families.
