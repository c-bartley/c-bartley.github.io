What I work on

My research sits at the intersection of speech technology and language endangerment. I develop methods that let small, under-resourced languages access the same AI capabilities that major languages take for granted — text-to-speech, automatic speech recognition, machine translation, and voice conversion — and I do this with a fraction of the training data that standard approaches require.

My primary language of focus is Manx Gaelic (Gaelg), a Celtic language from the Isle of Man that was declared extinct in 2009 but has since been the subject of one of the most remarkable language revitalisation efforts in the world.

Key areas

Low-resource speech technology

Building ASR, TTS, and voice conversion systems that work with limited data. My Interspeech 2026 paper demonstrates bootstrapping speech recognition from short-form corpora for endangered languages.

Diffusion-based voice conversion

Developing Grad-VC, a diffusion-based voice conversion framework built on Grad-TTS/DiffVC, designed for non-parallel training with word-pair supervision — removing the need for forced alignment in low-resource settings.

Gaelg AI platform

Created and maintain gaelgai.im, a public web platform providing AI-powered tools for Manx Gaelic: speech synthesis, recognition, translation, and audio timestamping.

Celtic & multilingual NLP

Working across the Celtic language family and other endangered languages to develop transferable, multilingual approaches to speech and language processing.
